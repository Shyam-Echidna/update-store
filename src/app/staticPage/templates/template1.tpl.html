Azaleas for Indoors

Written By Margaret Purcell, Senior Horticulturist

Azaleas are undoubtedly one of the most beautiful and prolific blooming plants available for your home. It is not unusual for indoor azaleas to be so covered with blooms that you have to search for the foliage!

Although they are close relatives to the rhododendrons and azleas that bloom in your outdoor gardens, indoor azaleas are not winter-hardy in our area. All the hardy azaleas in our climate are deciduous, losing their leaves every winter. Indoor azaleas are actually evergreen and grow prolifically in the south.

Tips for Choosing Indoor Azaleas

To choose quality azaleas that will bloom for several weeks, look for a plant with few blooms and lots of tight buds just beginning to show color. These leaves should be rich dark green and the plant should be compact and full.

Caring for Indoor Azaleas
Light Requirements
  • While blooming, keep it close to a window where it can receive at least 6 hours of indirect sunlight per day.
Watering
  • Azaleas prefer being consistently moist.
  • They may require daily watering if your home is warm and dry.
  • Avoid leaving water in the saucer for more than an hour.
Temperature Requirements
  • When in bloom, keep them at around 55-60 degrees; blooms don't last as long in warmer temperatures.
  • Azaleas will tolerate a wide range of temperatures, from near freezing to 90o plus.
  • Low temperatures trigger re-blooming.
Humidity
  • Azaleas prefer high humidity.
  • Use a humidity tray to provide moisture without harming blooms.
Fertilizer
  • Azaleas are acid lover; be sure your water is not too alkaline.
  • Slightly acidify your plant by adding 1 tablespoon vinegar to each gallon of water.
  • Azaleas perform best when given light applications of food weekly.
  • Use a water-soluble, acid based fertilizer at half strength.
  • Let the plant rest without feeding for 1-2 months, mid summer.
Soil
  • Azaleas prefer any well-drained peat-based potting mix, such as Bachman's Mighty Earth Potting Soil.
  • Report in late summer or early fall.
  • If possible, use clay pot.
Grooming
  • As the blooms fade, remove the flowers.
  • If it needs pruning, it should be done immediately after bloom.
  • An occasional shower to clean off the dust.
Propagation
  • Azaleas are not usually propagated at home.
  • Commercially they are propagated by either tissue culture or rooted softwood cuttings.

Plant is shedding lots of leaves
Causes
  • Effect of dry soil, either past or present
Solutions
  • Make sure the plant is thoroughly watered.
  • Soak in a sink or bucket if plant has dried out.
  • Discard the plant if it has lost more than 1/3 of its leaves.

Leaves turn yellow, but leaf veins remain green
Causes
  • Iron deficiency
Solutions
  • Check to make sure the fertilizer you are using has iron.
